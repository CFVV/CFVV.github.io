About

I work at the intersection of machine learning and geospatial data, guided by one question: how can Earth observation and urban data become information that helps cities anticipate, withstand, and recover from disasters? Chile — seismic, coastal, and increasingly fire-prone — is both my home and my case study.

I came to computer science through cities. Trained first as an architect specialized in territorial urban projects, and as an elementary school teacher, I learned Python to automate design workflows and quickly found a larger use for it: analyzing the urban data behind the problems I had been drawing by hand. That path led to a Master's in Computer Science and to research in GeoAI. Read the longer story →

Now: GeoAI researcher at Pontificia Universidad Católica de Chile, working with CIGIDEN (Research Center for Integrated Disaster Risk Management) on satellite-based models to detect and anticipate informal settlement growth in Valparaíso, feeding the SIRVAL territorial risk platform. Also research assistant at the Millennium Institute for Foundational Research on Data (IMFD), Cities Group, studying which urban visual elements in street-view imagery correlate with geolocated crime in Santiago.

Before: M.Sc. in Engineering (Computer Science) at PUC — thesis with maximum distinction on urban representation learning; research internship at the National Institute of Informatics (NII), Tokyo, in Prof. Asanobu Kitamoto's GeoAI lab; student researcher at IMFD and Chile's National Center for Artificial Intelligence (CENIA).

Research

How can Earth-observation foundation models and multimodal GeoAI sustain dynamic models of urban exposure and vulnerability in multi-hazard contexts of the Global South — where labels are scarce and informal settlements change faster than any census?

Exposure mapping

Informal settlement detection & forecasting

A satellite early-warning system for informal settlement expansion: XGBoost change detection over Sentinel-2 mosaics (Google Earth Engine), with MODIS wildfire filtering and land-cover postprocessing. Improves field-survey targeting ~9× for TECHO-Chile and the regional government via SIRVAL.

Representation learning

Vector representations of cities

Learning urban embeddings from multimodal data — satellite imagery, street networks, sociodemographic attributes — for downstream urban tasks. M.Sc. thesis, maximum distinction.

Risk & decision support

Multi-hazard risk platforms

Contributing GeoAI components to SIRVAL, a public territorial-risk platform for Greater Valparaíso integrating earthquakes, tsunamis, wildfires and mass movements for decision-makers.

Urban perception & safety

Street-view imagery & crime

Building labeled datasets and testing segmentation and object-detection models on Santiago street-view imagery to identify which urban visual elements predict or correlate with geolocated crime (IMFD, Cities Group).

Software

Python library

SpatialCluster

A framework for acquiring, comparing, and visualizing urban clustering solutions — implements five spatial clustering methods (DMoN, GMM, KNN, SOM, and information-theoretic clustering), with evaluation metrics and map visualization. Created by Axel Reyes within the IMFD Cities Group; I contributed testing, documentation, and co-authored the SoftwareX paper. Companion library to our EPJ Data Science study.
